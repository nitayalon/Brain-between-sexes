Abstract

In this notebook we’ll present the results of sampling from synthetic mixture distributions (men and women) and the effect of full separation (male and female) on the llk ratio and p-value.

Methods

We create synthetic observations by sampling from a mixture model of 2 normals with known means, variance and mixture proportions. Under the null hypothesis we assume the following mixture proportions: \[ p = 1\\ q = 0 \] While under the alternative we assume: \[ p << 1\\ q >> 0 \] After the data is sampled we normalized it to have a mean of 0 and variance of 1.
